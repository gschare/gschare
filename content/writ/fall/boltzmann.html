Boltzmann Brain

by Prof. Mueller

Boltzmann argued the following: over a sufficiently long time, random fluxuations in even very low energy particles could result in the spontaneous formation of complex structures.

Think of it like this. When we see shapes in clouds, there is no structured process giving them form. It is only the random, turbulent mixing of air and water at altitude. But as clouds form all of the world all day every day, would it be so surprising to imagine that once every now and then, by sheer random chance, a cloud forms a near-perfect image of your own face? What then, if its lips moved like yours do, albeit through coincidence and not intention? What if it appeared to speak silent words of wisdom?

A complex pattern is complex regardless of its origins. Meaning is something we perceive, not an inherent property that hides as metaphysical truth behind the look of things. The appearance of a mind, given sufficient complexity, is indistinguishable from a “real”, evolved mind. That is the Boltzmann brain.
