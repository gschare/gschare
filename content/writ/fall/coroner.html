Tom Henker's Autopsy

Item: coroner's report (transcript)
Date: 10/29/2000
Archived: 11/1/2000
Subject(s): Tom Henker

Transcript

Subject is 45 year old male, admitted dead on arrival to Pauling General Hospital. Presiding physician Dr. Dugme.

[…]

Evidence of death by asphyxiation. Surface ecchymosis consistent with hypothesis. No sign of strangulation. Airways intact.

[…]

Making thoracic incision…

[…]

Both lungs appear to have collapsed instantly. Checking history… subject has no history of lung disease, smoking, or pneumothorax. Subject is 5’7”; structural failure is unlikely to be correlated.

Cause of death concluded to be hypoxia.

Notes

The apparent subject, Mr. Tom Henker, is known to be alive at the time of writing. The relevant police incident report describes him choking in a restaurant and recovering completely.

Dr. Dugme was brought in and questioned about this report. He claims no memory of performing any autopsies on October 29th, and has never admitted a man under the name of Henker. Beyond this he was unwilling to cooperate. Other documents place him near Pauling Scrapyard and Metal Reclamation on the evening of October 29th.
