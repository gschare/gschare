Dear Analyst,

The October 29th Pauling Incident (codename: Fall Break) has motivated the formation of an incident response team made up of members of the Special Committee for the Investigation into the Evolution of Natural Constants (SCIENC).

The following is a short brief written to acquaint you with the situation and what we need from you.

Starting on October 31st, 2000, the Fall Break Archive Team has assembled documents from during and after the incident in an attempt to piece together the answer to our most pressing question.

We know that the cause of the incident was an officially unrecognized particle collision experiment at the Pauling Power Plant (internally known as the Pauling Quantum Energy Research Center). According to the supervising director, the experiment resulted in the creation of a miniature black hole. The experiment was swiftly aborted and the black hole evaporated, but between the 11:15 AM on October 29th and 11:42 AM the following morning, for 24 hours and 27 minutes, the townspeople of Pauling reported multiple phenomena consistent with a localized spacetime fracture (technically, a Hilbert Superposition). Please see our incident overview report and analysis for more details.

What we do not know at this time, and what we need your help in discerning, is what caused the phenomena to abruptly cease at 11:42 AM on October 30th. To help you deduce the answer to this question, we are providing you with partial access to the archived materials. We are unable to permit you full access due to security policies at SCIENC.

To get started, have a look at these cases, and go from there:

  • The Case of Marcia’s Picnic
  • The Case of the Ayahuasca Support Group

Please note that the documents assembled in this archive exhibit numerous factual inconsistencies, but in most cases, we have found no evidence of willful deception or manipulation. We believe that on October 29th, reality split into an unknown number of parallel timelines, and when they recombined on the morning of October 30th, our reality became something resembling a weighted random sample of events across the whole distribution of possible universes. That is to say, if you went out for milk in half of the realities and stayed home in the other half, you might remember staying home and yet a fresh carton of milk appears in your fridge.
