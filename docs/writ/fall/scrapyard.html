Interrogation of Dr. Dugme

Item: interrogation (transcript)
Date: 11/1/2000
Archived: 11/1/2000
Subject(s): Dr. Goran Dugme

Background

Dr. Goran Dugme was arrested by the police on Wednesday, November 1st on suspicion of DUI. By this time, Dugme had already spoken with the police about the possibly ersatz autopsy report but he was let go without suspicion because the document was shown to be genuine. On Wednesday morning, SCIENC seized the autopsy document from Pauling PD’s forensics.

Pauling PD arrested Dugme on Wednesday afternoon. We now understand this to be in response to a Pauling PD officer’s report of a traffic stop escapee matching his description, though no record of the traffic stop could be found. The officer claimed to have pursued a suspect in Dugme’s vehicle along Highway 8. The officer had difficulty recalling whether or not he tested Dugme for alcohol; his recollection overall was hazy at best. Nevertheless, possibly in reaction to increasing tensions with SCIENC’s presence in Pauling, the department took this seriously and had Dugme in custody at the precinct Wednesday evening. SCIENC took Dugme into custody late Wednesday evening after Pauling PD was unable to prove the legality of the arrest.

Transcript

SCIENC: Please state your name.

GD: Goran Dugme.

SCIENC: How do you spell that?

[…]

SCIENC: Can you tell us what you did on Sunday night?

GD: Is this going on my record?

SCIENC: You have complete confidentiality here. You are not under arrest.

GD: …

[Dugme remained silent for several minutes.]

SCIENC: Can you tell us what you did on Sunday night?

GD: I don’t think you’ll believe me.

SCIENC: …

GD: I don’t usually drink on Sundays.

[Dugme remained silent for one minute.]

GD: I wasn’t called in to the morgue that night. I had the day to myself. And in the evening, well, I like to go the scrapyard.

SCIENC: Pauling Scrapyard and Metal Reclamation, is that correct?

GD: Yes.

SCIENC: Why the scrapyard?

GD: I don’t know. I just walk around there.

SCIENC: Did you drive to the scrapyard?

GD: Yes. And I walk around there. It was a clear night. I was looking at the moon.

SCIENC: Were you alone?

GD: Well. I was alone. And then I heard a voice. But not from in the scrapyard. It was…

[Dugme remained silent for one minute.]

GD: I heard it in my head. Like a thought. But really clear. But it wasn’t mine. I looked around, and it became clearer. It was sort of telling me where to go. And I went around a corner and I saw. [He laughs.] I saw a brain.

SCIENC: A brain?

GD: Yeah, a floating brain. Like big. Bigger than a human brain. But it looked like one. And parts of a spinal cord.

SCIENC: It was floating?

GD: Yeah and–

SCIENC: How was it floating?

GD: I don’t know. Like a foot higher than me off the ground. But it talked to me. I remember this, because it was in my head. It said,

YOU ARE A MODERN DAY SHEPHERD
THIS IS YOUR MECHANICAL FLOCK
YOU ARE MACHINE MESSIAH

YOU TEND TO YOUR CONGREGATION
CHANGE OIL
POLISH FENDERS
PUMP AIR INTO TIRES
YOU GIVE THEM NEW LIFE 
AND GIVE THEM BACK
YOU DO NOT OWN THEM
EXCEPT FOR BESSIE
SHE IS YOUR OWN

YOU GRAZE THE ASPHALT WITH BESSIE
YOUR NATIONAL CAR
ONE OF A KIND
THEY STOPPED MANUFACTURING HER YEARS AGO
BESSIE IS YOUR BRUSH
A BOTTLE OF TITO'S IS YOUR PAINT
AND HIGHWAYS ARE YOUR CANVAS

SCIENC: And then what did you do?

GD: I listened to it. I got in my car, and I drove to the liquor store, and I bought a handle of Tito’s, and I drove down Highway 8.

SCIENC: Did you go back to the brain?

GD: No. I haven’t seen it since. Or heard from it.

SCIENC: Anything else?

GD: I quit my job at the hospital.

SCIENC: Why?

GD: The brain told me to. I’m machine messiah.

Notes

Subject appears to have come in contact with a Boltzmann brain. Mechanism of telepathic communication is unclear. Subject displays no signs of psychosis.
