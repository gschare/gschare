The Case of Tom Henker

Item: police report (internal)
Date: 10/29/2000
Archived: 10/31/2000
Subject(s): Tom Henker

Incident Report

Two officers responded to 911 call originating from landline at Cal Wok Chinese Restaurant at 7:38 PM. Tom Henker, 45, resident of Manzanita County, reported feeling normal when officers arrived and appears healthy following incident. No foul play suspected (see below). Restaurant temporarily closed pending food safety inspection. Mr. Henker referred to psychological services for treatment for possible stress disorder.

Caller (Mr. Jun Li) reported seeing Mr. Henker choking suddenly. Caller was at dinner with Mr. Henker on a business trip, along with two colleagues and one colleague of Mr. Henker from the Pauling Tractor Company. Bystanders reported Mr. Henker began to choke and signal for help.

Quote transcribed from audio recording of questioning Mr. Henker following incident:

It was like all the air in my lungs just disappeared and there was nothing for me to breathe. It was strange. I wasn’t even eating at the time - I didn’t choke on anything. I was in the middle of talking to Jun and Franklin about the merger and then all the air around me was gone. Then it was over and I could breathe again.

Officers responding at the scene asked Mr. Henker about his personal history.

No, well, I suppose I’ve been stressed about the acquisition but this has never happened before. I’ve been to this restaurant 6,022,140 times. [inaudible] What? Sorry, did I say that? I meant dozens of times.

Ongoing investigation into existence of coroner’s report of Mr. Henker’s body, dated Sunday, October 29th, 2000. During preliminary inspection, document appears to be genuine and not a forgery. Pauling General Hospital coroner reports no memory of performing an autopsy. Document currently under federal government seizure.

Notes

SCIENC dispatched a technical team to the scene to conduct an analysis of the phenomenon. They found abnormal spikes in the vorticity of the room’s air, indicating a possible flux anomaly.
